About

Physics League Across
Numerous Countries
for Kick-ass Students

PLANCKS is the premier international physics competition for bachelor's and master's students, organised by the International Association of Physics Students (IAPS). Each edition gathers the brightest young physicists from across the globe to compete, collaborate, and forge lasting scientific bonds.

Teams of three to four tackle rigorous theoretical problems spanning all domains of physics — classical mechanics, electromagnetism, quantum theory, statistical physics, relativity, astrophysics, and more — crafted by leading professors worldwide.

Beyond competition, PLANCKS is a five-day cultural and scientific exchange: symposia, laboratory visits, excursions, and an awards ceremony that celebrates excellence in physics education on an international stage.

The 2027 edition seeks to expand PLANCKS's reach by inviting students and teams from across all of Latin America, strengthening academic, cultural, and scientific exchange between participants from diverse countries. The official logo reflects this identity — integrating the scarlet macaw (Honduras's national bird), patterns inspired by Maya architecture and writing, the year 2027 in Maya numerals, and the reduced Planck constant ħ, one of the most iconic symbols of quantum mechanics.

A 3-hour theoretical exam of extraordinary depth, crafted by world-class physicists.

International Community

Delegates from 30+ nations form friendships and collaborations that last careers.

Cultural Immersion

Each edition explores the host country's history, landscape, and scientific heritage.

Host Country

The Heart of
Central America

Honduras — a land of volcanic mountains, ancient Maya civilisation, and extraordinary biodiversity — will host PLANCKS for the first time ever in Central America. A historic milestone in the competition's history.

The host city, San Pedro Sula, is the nation's industrial and economic heart, strategically located near the Caribbean coast and surrounded by Maya heritage, cloud forests, and colonial towns. Home to Copán — one of the great Maya ceremonial centres and a UNESCO World Heritage Site — and to coral reefs, rainforests, and vibrant cities, Honduras offers an incomparable cultural backdrop for an international scientific gathering.

Experience Honduras

Culture, Nature & Gastronomy

Beyond the competition, PLANCKS 2027 offers a rich cultural programme to discover the natural and cultural heritage of Honduras.

Cultural Programme

Tela & Lancetilla Botanical Garden

Visit the coastal town of Tela and its renowned Lancetilla Botanical Garden — one of the largest botanical gardens in Latin America.

Playa La Ensenada & Cayos Cochinos

Relax on the beaches of La Ensenada and explore the marine biodiversity of Cayos Cochinos, a protected archipelago.

Garífuna Community Visit

Experience the vibrant culture of the Garífuna people, recognised by UNESCO for their intangible cultural heritage.

Folk Dance & Music

Enjoy traditional Honduran folk dance performances and live music throughout the event.

Honduran Gastronomy

Baleada

Honduras's beloved national dish: a thick flour tortilla filled with refried beans, cheese, and your choice of toppings.

Sopa de Caracol

Traditional conch soup, a Caribbean coastal delicacy rich in coconut milk, spices, and fresh seafood.

Tourist Attractions

Laguna de los Micos

A lush coastal lagoon near Tela, home to howler monkeys and rich birdlife.

Cataratas Pulhapanzak

A spectacular 43-metre waterfall on the Lindo River, one of Honduras's most impressive natural wonders.

Fortaleza de San Fernando

An 18th-century Spanish fortress in Omoa, overlooking the Caribbean coast.

Organising Team

Organising Committee

PLANCKS Honduras 2027 is co-organised by IAPS Honduras, IAPS Guatemala and IAPS Costa Rica — a trinational effort of physics students from three Central American countries, supported by IAPS internationally.

PLANCKS 2027 will take place across two iconic venues in San Pedro Sula. The competition and academic sessions are hosted at the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de Honduras (UNAH) campus, while the opening and closing ceremonies will be held at the historic Teatro José Francisco Saybe.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about registering, competing, and attending PLANCKS 2027 Honduras.

PLANCKS is open to bachelor's and master's students of physics or closely related disciplines. Teams consist of three to four members. Most countries hold national preliminaries — the winners represent their nation at the international competition.
Each country's IAPS member committee organises a national preliminary. Countries without an existing committee can contact our organising team directly — we actively support new national committees, especially across Latin America and the Caribbean.
Registration covers accommodation for all event nights, transport between venue and accommodation, all meals during the event, the cultural programme and excursions, access to all scientific sessions, and both opening and closing ceremonies. Flights are not included.
Yes. Observer registration is available for students, professors, and interested parties who wish to attend the symposia, social events, and cultural programme without competing. Details will be published when registration opens in 2026.

We are actively seeking sponsorship to provide travel grants, particularly for teams from countries with limited resources and especially from Latin America and the Caribbean. Details will be published alongside the official registration opening.
